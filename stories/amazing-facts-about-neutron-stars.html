What is a Neutron Star?

Discover 6 fascinating NASA facts about these cosmic objects!

Fact 1

Neutron stars are the remnants of massive star explosions, collapsing under their own gravity.

Fact 2

They are incredibly dense – a teaspoon of neutron star material weighs billions of tons.

Fact 3

Neutron stars can rotate up to 600 times per second, making them one of the fastest rotating objects in the universe.

Fact 4

Their magnetic fields are millions of times stronger than Earth's, influencing their surroundings significantly.

Fact 5

Neutron stars' surface temperature can exceed 1 million degrees Celsius.

Fact 6

Neutron stars are the densest objects in the universe, other than black holes.

Exploration

Neutron stars can be explored using powerful telescopes like Hubble and the Chandra X-ray Observatory.

Did You Know?

The first neutron star was discovered in 1967 by Jocelyn Bell Burnell.

The Future

Future space missions will continue to study neutron stars to learn more about the universe.

Conclusion

Neutron stars are some of the most fascinating objects in our universe. Stay tuned for more amazing space facts!
